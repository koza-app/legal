2. Scope of Service

Koza is a digital tracking and support application that provides parents with the following features:

  • Baby feeding (breastfeeding, bottle, pumping, solid food), sleep, diaper changes, and growth tracking
  • Turkey vaccination schedule and vaccination reminders
  • Developmental milestone tracking (motor, language, social, cognitive)
  • Illness, medication, and solid food allergy records
  • Mother's health tracking: mood, weight, postpartum recovery, postnatal depression screening (EPDS)
  • AI-powered daily insights, weekly reports, Q&A assistant, and nutrition suggestions
  • PDF report generation for pediatricians
  • Synchronized data sharing with a partner (spouse)
Important: Koza is NOT a medical device, diagnostic tool, or treatment application. Application content, AI outputs, EPDS screening results, and growth charts are for informational and tracking purposes only. Always consult a pediatrician, gynecologist, or relevant healthcare professional when making medical decisions.

3.2 EPDS Postnatal Depression Screening Test

The Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) included in the application is a validated clinical screening tool but does not constitute a medical diagnosis. Test results are indicative only. A high-risk result does not necessarily mean you must consult a mental health specialist; a low-risk result does not mean depression is absent.

If you experience any psychological symptoms or feel concerned, please consult a psychiatrist or clinical psychologist. In crisis situations:

  • Alo SABİM 182 — Ministry of Health contact line (Turkey)
  • 112 — Emergency line (life-threatening situations or suicidal thoughts)

3.3 Vaccination Schedule

The vaccination schedule presented in the application is based on the Turkish Ministry of Health's expanded immunization program. However, each child's health condition may vary. Always consult your pediatrician for vaccination decisions.

3.4 Growth Charts

Growth percentile charts are based on World Health Organization (WHO) reference data. Percentile results alone do not indicate nutritional disorders, developmental delays, or any other health conditions. Consult your pediatrician for evaluation.
